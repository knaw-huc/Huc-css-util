What are the Dutch Prize Papers

The Prize Papers are documents seized by British navy and privateers from enemy ships in the period 1652-1815. The documents contain shipâ€™s journals, lists of cargo, accounts, plantation lists and interrogations of crew members, business- and private letters.

Organisation of the archive

The papers are kept in the archive of the High Court of Admiralty in The National Archives in Kew (London). Approximately a quarter of the Prize Papers originates from Dutch ships.
